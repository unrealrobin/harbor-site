You're one of the first developers shipping Harbor inside a real game on Steam, which makes you a pretty small group. Here's a real note before you start clicking through menus.

## Program at a glance

- **Audience:** the first few developers shipping Harbor inside a real Steam game.
- **Offer:** six months free, full access to the current modules.
- **What we ask:** biweekly feedback calls (about 20–30 minutes), and a heads-up that month six transitions to a paid subscription.
- **Contact:** robin@paracosm.gg. Put `blocker` in the subject line for anything stopping your integration.

## The short version

I built Harbor because I kept watching indie devs ship updates into a void. You spend a week on a patch, hit publish on a Steam post most of your players will never see, then wait for the few who happen to scroll through their library that week to notice. The communication tools Steam and Epic give us are genuinely bad, and nothing fills that gap at the moment that matters: the moment a player has decided to actually play your game tonight.

Harbor is my attempt at that. A small launcher that opens before your game, shows whatever you want to say, then gets out of the way. No store, no accounts, no fingerprint of mine on what your players see. It's your launcher.

## What you get

Full access to everything Harbor currently ships, free for **six months** from your first login:

- **Patch Notes** — formatted markdown changelogs at session start.
- **News** — announcements, dev posts, anything you want in front of your active players.
- **Dev Comments** — shorter "what we're working on right now" notes.
- The Harbor launcher binary itself (`harbor.exe`), branded as yours: your logo, colors, fonts, and name in the title bar.
- Unlimited content pushes through the CLI.
- CDN delivery for everything you push.
- A direct line to me for anything that breaks, anything that's confusing, or anything you wish Harbor did.

There are no usage limits during the six months. Ship as much content as you want, to as many players as you have.

## What we ask in return

**Biweekly check-ins.** Roughly every two weeks, a short call (20 to 30 minutes) to walk through what's working, what isn't, and what you'd change. These calls are how Harbor gets better. The first one or two matter most; after that we'll find the cadence that works for you.

**A heads-up about month six.** Harbor is a paid subscription product. When the six months are up, continuing to use Harbor means moving to one of the regular tiers (currently $20–$40 per month depending on which modules you're using). You're hearing it now, in writing, so it's never a surprise. If at month five you decide Harbor isn't earning its keep, we'll part on good terms and I'll help you remove it cleanly. If it is, the transition is one email and a payment link.

That's it. No NDAs, no exclusivity, no "you have to keep it running for X months." You can uninstall on day three if it turns out Harbor isn't for you. The whole point of running this with a small group is to find where Harbor falls short while I can still do something about it.

## How to reach me

The fastest way is email: **robin@paracosm.gg**. I read everything, usually within a day. For bugs, paste what you're seeing. If something is fully broken and blocking you, put `blocker` in the subject and I'll prioritize it.

## What to do next

Three things, in order:

1. Read the [Quickstart](/docs/quickstart) — gets you from zero to "harbor.exe is shipping with my game" in about an hour.
2. Read the [Customization & Content Guide](/docs/customization) — branding, theme, modules, the assets to have ready, and the pre-submission checklist.
3. Run `harbor` and walk through `init`. The CLI is interactive; pick options from the menu and it'll guide you.

When you've got the launcher running with your game wired up, email me. I'd love to see a screenshot.

Thanks for taking the bet on something this early.

— Robin
